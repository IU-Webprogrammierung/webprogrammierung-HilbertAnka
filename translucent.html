Translucent Scenic Painting

Translucent painting involves working on special materials such as scrim, shirting, or opera film. The aim is to create effects that only fully unfold their impact when backlit. With rear illumination, spatial and atmospheric transparency effects are created. This technique requires particular precision, as corrections are limited and opaque overpainting is not possible.
